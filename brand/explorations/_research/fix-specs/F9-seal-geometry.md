# F9 — Seal: medallions sit ON the ring stroke; weak hub hierarchy; collapses small

## Root cause

The 12-medallion seal has three coupled geometry faults, present in **both**
seal source files:

1. **Medallions straddle the ring stroke.** The medallion orbit radius equals
   the ring radius exactly. In `rtam-12-medallion-seal.svg` (viewBox 360,
   center 180): ring `r=160`; each medallion is `<circle cx="180" cy="20" r="8">`
   so its center sits at orbit radius `180 − 20 = 160` — identical to the ring
   radius. With ring `stroke-width=2` the stroke occupies `r∈[159,161]` and the
   `r=8` medallion occupies `r∈[152,168]`, so the ring line passes straight
   through the middle of every bead. The bead reads as "bisected by a wire,"
   not as a discrete element with a relationship to the ring.
   `rtam-12-medallion-pattern.svg` repeats the identical fault at half scale:
   ring `r=90`, orbit `90`, medallion `r=4.5` on a `stroke-width=1.25` line.

2. **Hub vs medallion hierarchy too close.** Center bindu `r=10` vs medallion
   `r=8` → linear ratio **1.25×**, area ratio **1.56×**. A 1.25× radius bump is
   below the threshold at which the eye reliably reads "this is the center, those
   are satellites"; the hub looks like a thirteenth, slightly fat medallion that
   happens to be in the middle. The pattern tile is worse: hub `r=5.5` vs
   medallion `r=4.5` → **1.22×**.

3. **Collapses below ~96px.** Driven by (1) and (2): when the whole mark is a
   few-pixel-tall ring, a bead that already straddles the stroke disappears into
   it, and a hub only 1.25× a bead is indistinguishable from one. There is no
   stated minimum clear size, so the seal is shipped at sizes where it stops
   reading as "hub + 12 beads + ring."

## Exact change   (implementable blind: exact attributes, exact numbers, exact replacement text)

### Shared geometry rule (the durable core — instantiate per file)

Given a ring of radius `R` and stroke width `W`, and a chosen medallion radius
`m`, place the 12 medallions **inside** the ring on an orbit of radius:

```
ring_inner = R − W/2
gap        = m                      # one full bead-width of clear air
orbit      = ring_inner − m − gap   # = R − W/2 − 2m
hub        = round(1.7 × m)         # hub radius
```

Medallions are 12 copies at 30° spacing, generated by placing one circle at
`cy = center − orbit` and rotating `0,30,…,330°` about the center (the existing
`transform="rotate(k·30 cx cy)"` idiom is kept verbatim).

### C. Minimum clear size (durable rule → spec + guidelines)

Add to `brand/spec/brand.json` (and surface in usage guidelines): the seal has a
**minimum clear size of 96px**. Below 96px, use the standalone bindu mark or the
wordmark instead of the seal. Rationale in "Parameters resolved" below.

## Parameters resolved   (every number FINAL with one-line rationale)

| Param | Seal | Pattern tile | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ring radius `R` | 160 | 90 | Unchanged — preserves the seal's footprint and the 360/240 viewBoxes; the fault was never the ring size. **Durable.** |
| Ring stroke `W` | **3** (was 2) | **1.5** (was 1.25) | A hairline-2 ring is the first thing to vanish on downscale; 3 (and the proportional 1.5) is a confident-but-still-fine line that survives to 96px. Tile value = `3 × 0.5625 ≈ 1.69`, rounded to 1.5 for a crisp sub-pixel-free stroke at tile scale. **Durable.** |
| Medallion radius `m` | **7.5** (was 8) | **4.0** (was 4.5) | Slightly reduced so the orbit can move inside the ring and still leave a full bead-width of air; visual bead weight is essentially unchanged. **Durable.** |
| Orbit radius | **143.5** (was 160) | **81.25** (was 90) | `R − W/2 − 2m`. Places the bead's outer edge a full bead-width (`gap = m`) inside the ring's inner stroke edge → unambiguous "inside the ring," not "on it." Edge-to-edge clearance = 7.5px (seal) / 4.0px (tile). **Durable.** |
| Hub radius | **13** (was 10) | **7** (was 5.5) | `round(1.7 × m)` = 12.75→13 (seal, 1.73×) and 6.8→7 (tile, 1.75×). 1.7× linear = ~2.9× area: the hub now dominates a single bead decisively while staying a dot, not a disc that fights the ring. Below ~1.5× the hub reads as a 13th bead; above ~2× it starts to look like a small filled center medallion. **Durable.** |
| Medallion `cy` | 36.5 | 38.75 | `center − orbit`; mechanical. |
| Min clear size | **96px** | n/a (decorative field) | Measured (see Proof): at 96px the hub clearly dominates, 12 beads are distinct, ring is continuous, bead↔ring gap is visible. At 64px the hub-vs-bead hierarchy and the 1px ring degrade to "parseable only if you already know the mark." 96px is the honest floor for the seal reading as hub+12+ring. **Durable.** |

Gap rule `gap = m` (one bead-width of air) and `hub = 1.7 m` are the durable,
form-only ratios; they reproduce both files from their ring radius and are what
the generator/spec should encode (not the raw pixel numbers).

- `brand/explorations/_research/fix-specs/proofs/F9-seal-before-after.png`

- `brand/explorations/_research/fix-specs/proofs/F9-seal-smallsize.png`

  the ring to a fragile 1px hairline — it still *parses* but the hub-vs-bead
  hierarchy is collapsing; this is the visual evidence for the 96px floor (the
  seal is degraded, not clean, at 64px — we do **not** claim it reads well there).

  — pattern tile BEFORE vs AFTER: same correction at tile scale; beads pulled
  inside the ring, hub enlarged, and the beads stay well within the tile (no

## Verification steps   (how US-16 re-audit will confirm the fix)

1. **Orbit clearance (geometry, not pixels).** For each file, read ring `R`,
   `W`, medallion `m`, and medallion `cy`. Compute orbit `= center − cy` and
   assert `orbit + m ≤ R − W/2 − (m − ε)` — i.e. the bead's outer edge clears the
   ring's inner stroke edge by ≥ ~one bead-width. Expect seal clearance 7.5px,
   tile 4.0px. Fails if any bead touches/crosses the stroke.
2. **Hub hierarchy.** Assert `hub / m ≥ 1.6` in both files (seal 13/7.5=1.73,
   tile 7/4=1.75).
3. **Min-size legibility.** Rasterize the seal (cairosvg is faithful — these are
   pure-geometry SVGs, no live text) at 96px on the ivory ground; confirm 12
   distinct bead blobs in the orbit annulus, a hub blob strictly larger than any
   bead blob, and a continuous ring. Confirm `brand.json` records
   `seal.minClearSizePx = 96` and the guideline page states the <96px fallback.
4. **Tiling.** Rasterize the pattern; assert no bead pixel lies within 1px of any
   240-tile boundary (outermost bead extent 85.47 « 120).
5. **Meaning text.** Grep the seal `<desc>` for "twelve Ādityas" / "solar months"
   and assert the old "signs of the zodiac, hours of the day" multi-reading is
   gone; assert "Jyotirliṅga" appears only as the reserved future-temple note.
6. **No spelling/filename drift.** Confirm filenames and all "Rtambhareshvara"/
   "ṚTAM" strings are untouched (this fix changes none).

## Residual risk

- **Cinzel/Phase-2 independence:** none — the seal contains no letterforms, so a
  letterform swap cannot regress this geometry.
- **64px and below:** explicitly out of scope by the 96px floor; if a downstream
  use *must* render the seal at 64px (e.g. a fixed favicon slot), it should use
  the bindu mark instead — flagged so the re-audit doesn't reopen "collapses
  small" as a seal-geometry bug.
- **Stroke-width on hi-DPI print:** `W=3` at 360 units is ~0.83% of the viewBox;
  at very large print sizes this is a deliberately fine line. If a future
  large-format use wants a heavier ring, that is a scale-specific override, not a
  change to this rule.
- **Tile-edge framing:** the pattern proof shows a single tile on a solid ground;
  in the real repeating field neighboring tiles are 240 apart, so the 34.5px
  per-tile margin guarantees no inter-tile collision. Confirmed by the tiling
  check (step 4), but only single-tile rendered here.

## Durable or provisional

**Durable.** All values are pure geometric form (circles, radii, ratios) with no
dependency on the Cinzel letterforms. The ratio rules — medallions inside the
ring with `gap = m` of clear air, `hub = 1.7 m`, ring `stroke = 3` at 360 units /
proportional at tile scale, and the 96px minimum clear size — survive any
Phase-2 letterform change unchanged and are what the spec/generator should encode.
